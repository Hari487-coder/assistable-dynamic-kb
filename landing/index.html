Running in about five minutes

  1. Deploy it.One click creates your own instance on Render's free plan. The encryption key generates itself and the address is detected, so there is nothing to configure.
  2. Claim it.Open your instance and create the first account. Sign-ups close behind you, so the instance is yours alone, and so is the data.
  3. Connect your data.Paste your Assistable key, add a spreadsheet, feed, website or database, and tick which assistants get it. The tool is created for you.

About the free plan. Render's free tier sleeps after fifteen idle minutes and wipes its disk on redeploys, which is fine while you are trying it. For real customer calls, the always-free VM path in the docs is one command and keeps its data.

Questions

What do I need before I start?
Two accounts: Render, which is free and hosts your instance, and the Assistable account you already have. No card.
What access does it need to my Assistable account?
Four permissions and no others: assistants:list, tools:create, tools:update, and optionally tools:delete for cleanup. It never touches your contacts, calls or billing.
Does it work on phone calls?
Yes. The same tool serves voice and chat, and every answer carries a ready-to-speak sentence so the agent reads rather than improvises.
What can I connect?
Spreadsheet uploads, feed URLs in JSON, CSV or XML, a price table on one of your own pages, your whole website, or a read-only Postgres database.
What does it cost to run?
Nothing. No paid APIs, no database bill, no vector store. That constraint is also why it is fast: a lookup never leaves your instance.
