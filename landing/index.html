1

Create a free Render account & deploy

One click. Sign in with Google or GitHub — Render hosts your personal instance for free with a secure HTTPS address. Nothing to configure: the encryption key generates itself, the URL is auto-detected.

Just signed up? Render sometimes drops you on an empty dashboard after account creation — come back here and click the deploy button once more while logged in; you'll see a "New Blueprint" screen with everything pre-filled. Name it, click Deploy Blueprint, and wait ~2-3 minutes.

2

Open your new Live KB and create your account

When Render finishes (~2 minutes), open your instance's URL. The first account becomes the owner and sign-ups close automatically — it's yours alone, and so is the data: everything lives in one file on your instance.

3

Follow the in-app setup

Paste your Assistable API key (it needs only assistants:list, tools:create, tools:update — nothing else), add your data (CSV, feed, website, or database), tick your assistants — the tool is created in your Assistable account automatically. Paste one paragraph into your assistant's prompt, test it live on the same page, done.

Free-tier note: Render's free plan sleeps after 15 idle minutes and its disk resets on redeploys — great for trying it out. For real customer traffic, the always-free VM path (one command, permanent disk) in the self-hosting guide is the recommended $0 production home.

What accounts do I need?

Two: Render (free, hosts your instance) and your existing Assistable account (for the API key). The VM option swaps Render for Oracle Cloud.

Does it work on phone calls?

Yes — the same tool serves voice and chat automatically, and every answer includes a ready-to-speak summary the voice agent reads aloud.

What data can I connect?

CSV uploads, feed URLs (JSON/CSV/XML — every DMS and store platform can export one), whole websites, and read-only Postgres/Supabase.

What does it cost?

Nothing. No paid APIs, no paid database, free hosting tiers. The zero-cost design is also why it's fast — a lookup never leaves your instance.
