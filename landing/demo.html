Internal test bench

Ask the agent something only live data knows.

This page embeds the Assistable chat widget. The assistant behind it is connected to a Live KB instance, so its answers about prices come from a data source that syncs on a schedule, not from anything typed into its prompt.

Try these

Click one to copy it, then paste into the chat bubble at the bottom right.

  • What's bright copper going for in London?
  • How much do you pay for heavy brass in Manchester?
  • Do you buy lead?
  • What's your cheapest grade in Birmingham?
  • Do you take titanium?

What a good answer looks like

A specific number, tied to a grade and a place. Something like "Bright Copper in London is £7.20 per kilo." The price is read out of the live source at the moment you ask.

The last question is the honest-failure test: titanium is not in the data, so the assistant should say it doesn't have that rather than inventing a price.

How to tell it actually used the live data

  1. Ask one of the questions above.
  2. Open your Live KB portal and go to the source page.
  3. Under What customers asked the question appears within seconds, with the number of answers it found and how long the lookup took.

If the answer sounds right but nothing shows in that log, the assistant answered from its prompt or an old static knowledge base, not from live data.

Changing a price proves it end to end. Edit one price in the source, press Refresh data now, then ask the same question again. The new number should come back on the next message.
